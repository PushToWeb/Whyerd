What are magic truffles?

Maybe you have heard of people going on a trip with magic/psychedelic mushrooms, but what are exactly magic truffles?

There are around 180 species of magic mushrooms containing the psychedelic compounds psilocybin and psilocin. Out of all these, there are 4 which forms sclerotia instead of the fruiting body: the “mushroom” if some conditions are met. These are the Mexicana, Pajaritos, Tampanesis, and Atlantis. Sclerotia is a mass of mycelium which is formed underground but contains the exact same psychedelic compounds. In 2007, magic mushrooms were banned in the Netherlands, but truffles remained legal, being the only permitted way to consume the psychedelic. The reason why magic truffles were left off this prohibition is uncertain, but many people believe that this is because its potency is less than the mushroom’s. However, the trip it causes does not differ.
